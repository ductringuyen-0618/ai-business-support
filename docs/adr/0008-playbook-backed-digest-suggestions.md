# Weekly Digest suggestions: LLM picks from a curated Playbook of Patterns

The weekly Digest's top-3 suggested actions are generated by an LLM that picks the most relevant 3 Patterns from a curated Playbook of remediation patterns (~30–60 entries, keyed by Theme) and tailors them with specifics quoted from the actual Reviews in the week. The Playbook lives in the repo (`/lib/digest/playbook.ts`), is versioned with code, and is evolved by PR.

## Why

Open-ended LLM ideation is high-variance — sometimes brilliant, often generic ("provide better service"). The first time a Business owner reads "improve customer satisfaction" in their Monday Digest, they unsubscribe. A Playbook shifts the LLM's task from *generation* (high variance) to *selection + tailoring* (low variance), which is what LLMs do reliably. The Playbook also acts as a quality floor: every Pattern is a real action a human curated as worth-recommending, so the worst Digest output is still "decent advice expressed clearly", not "generic AI fluff".

Pure rules ("if X happens, suggest Y") are too rigid and produce identical-feeling Digests across Businesses. Deferring suggestions to v2 forfeits goal 5 of the product brief.

The Playbook is also a data-collection vehicle: tracking which Patterns get acted on over time becomes the foundation for an eventual outcome-tracking feature ([deferred future work](./0001-latency-budget-and-ingestion-model.md) — once the product has 2+ months of data per Business).

## Pattern shape

Each Pattern entry contains:

- `id` — stable slug for tracking
- `themes` — one or more Theme keys this Pattern applies to
- `verticals` — optional filter (e.g. `["restaurant", "cafe"]`); empty means universal
- `title` — short imperative ("Add an online queue")
- `body` — 1–2 sentences expanding the action
- `signals` — short note on when this Pattern is most relevant ("when wait_time complaints spike on Friday/Saturday evenings")

The Digest LLM receives the week's classified Reviews + the Playbook filtered by the Business's vertical, picks the 3 most relevant Patterns, and rewrites each with specifics quoted from the actual Reviews ("5 Reviews this week mentioned slow drinks on Friday — here's what we suggest…"). It must also recognise good weeks and select from a positive-reinforcement Pattern set rather than always defaulting to "fix something".

## Consequences

- The Playbook is a load-bearing product artifact. PRs that change it run the [evaluate-skill](../../.claude/skills/evaluate-skill/SKILL.md) methodology on a fixed set of synthetic Digest scenarios.
- The Playbook must include a `positive_reinforcement` section so good weeks generate "celebrate this strength" suggestions, not awkward "fix this" ones.
- A Business with 0 Reviews in the week skips the Digest entirely. Don't send "nothing to say" emails.
- When the Playbook is small (single-digit Patterns), the LLM has too little to choose from and the Digest feels repetitive across weeks. The first month after launch needs catalogue-growth as a priority.
- When the Playbook grows past ~100 Patterns, prompt-context pressure begins to bite. At that point, switch to per-vertical sub-Playbooks selected before the LLM call.
- The outcome-tracking feature (track which Pattern Operators acted on; correlate with Theme movement next month) is a natural fast-follow but is not in MVP.
